week ten: put it in a pocket

This assignment was completed on October 30, 2023

My goal for this weekly assignment was to experiment with the 8S-2 loom in the ATLAS equipment room and try to learn two layer weaving structures.

draft

The draft that I used for the baby wolf was the draft that was posted as an example. I did this because last minute I had to change my loom plans to baby wolf, and I didn't have enough time to remake a draft. I happened to (luckily) love the draft pattern, and as a complete beginner I chose to go that route and I am really happy with it.

set up

When starting to set up, I found issues with the tensions of the warp due to other classmates weaves. I didn't know how to fix this at first, so there were a lot of different unintensional floats within my weave at first. I later figured out how to fix this by running my finger along the warp to get some of the warps unstuck, which worked for me.

details

I really enjoyed some of the sections close up on my swatch. I was really excited about the different twill sections, I thought it was really cool. I also loved that I picked weft threads that were about the same thickness as the warp because I enjoyed the lack of variation, which I think made the pattern easier to see.

final swatch

In the end of the 2 hours, I was surprised with how much I was able to weave. I was excited to see that I was able to fix a ripped warp, as well as troubleshoot other problems that I had not yet experienced. I am much more inspired for my final project, and I would like to draft a different pattern for the baby wolf floor loom in the future.

reflection

As a whole, the loom was not my favorite in comparison to the floor loom. I found the floor loom to be much more intuitive and fun to use, so I will try to work on there from now on. I would like to try the pocket again in the future as I think that two layer structures are really important to understand.
